HOTEL SERI MALAYSIA, SUNGAI PETANI

Hotel Seri Malaysia Sungai Petani is a well-known 3-star property that offers simple, no-frills accommodation right in the heart of town. While the hotel has a slightly dated charm, its unbeatable location near the KTM train station and its highly affordable rates make it a popular pick for budget-conscious travelers.

HOTEL EXTERIOR & GROUNDS

Featuring the signature look of the classic Malaysian hospitality brand, the exterior of Hotel Seri Malaysia Sungai Petani blends a traditional high-roofed lobby entrance with a long, colorful main block. Surrounded by convenient, open-air parking and pocket gardens, the grounds offer a functional layout that makes checking in straightforward and stress-free.

RECEPTION & LOBBY

The lobby at Hotel Seri Malaysia Sungai Petani serves as a functional and welcoming gateway to the rest of the property. Featuring an open-plan layout with plenty of comfortable seating arrangements, it provides a cozy spot for family members to wait during the check-in and check-out procedures. The reception desk is manned by helpful local staff who are quick to offer assistance and answer questions about the surrounding area. Though the overall interior design is a bit dated and simple, the entire reception hall is kept clean, tidy, and organized, setting a pleasant and reliable tone for a budget-friendly stay.

ACCOMMODATION

The hotel features a large inventory of 74 rooms and apartments that can comfortably accommodate up to 252 guests simultaneously. The selection includes premium VIP Suites with lounge seating for couples or executives, and comfortable Deluxe Queen and Twin rooms tailored for weekend travelers and corporate guests. A rare standout feature for the property is its multi-level apartment units across levels one to four, which come fully furnished with dedicated family living rooms and multiple bathrooms for large staycations.

ROOM REVIEW

The rooms at Hotel Seri Malaysia Sungai Petani are widely praised for being large, clean, and highly affordable, though they feature older, traditional decor that shows the property's age. Guests appreciate the spacious layouts and well-kept linens, but many note that the attached bathrooms feel dated with older tiling and basic fixtures. Furthermore, because the building is only two stories high, it lacks an elevator, meaning guests staying on the upper floor must use the stairs to access their rooms.
